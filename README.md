# Consumer Buying Behaviour Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes consumer buying behaviour in India's online shopping market. The study focuses on understanding the factors that influence online purchase decisions, including pricing, product reviews, brand preference, shopping frequency, platform choice, and social media influence.

The objective of this project is to provide valuable insights into customer preferences and help businesses improve their marketing and sales strategies through data-driven decision-making.

---

## Objectives

- Understand online shopping behaviour.
- Identify key factors influencing buying decisions.
- Analyze the impact of reviews, pricing, and social media on consumer purchases.

---

## Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel
- Survey Analysis
- Data Visualization
- Charts and Graphs

---

## Survey Parameters

The study evaluates the following factors:

- Price Sensitivity
- Product Quality
- Brand Preference
- Online Shopping Frequency
- Preferred Shopping Platforms
- Review Importance
- Social Media Influence

---

## Key Findings

### Price is the Most Important Factor

Most respondents prioritize price when making online purchase decisions. Discounts and affordability play a significant role in influencing consumer behaviour.

### Online Shopping is Common

The majority of respondents shop online occasionally or frequently, indicating strong adoption of e-commerce platforms.

### Amazon is the Preferred Platform

Amazon emerged as the most preferred online shopping platform among respondents, followed by other major marketplaces.

### Reviews Influence Purchases

Most consumers check product reviews before making a purchase. Positive reviews increase trust and influence buying decisions.

### Social Media Has Limited Influence

While social media affects some purchase decisions, most respondents reported that it has a lower impact compared to pricing and reviews.

---

## Recommendations

- Focus on competitive pricing and attractive discounts.
- Improve product ratings and customer reviews.
- Strengthen digital marketing strategies.
- Build customer trust through transparency and quality service.

---

## Conclusion

The study concludes that price, convenience, and customer reviews are the most influential factors affecting online buying behaviour. Businesses that focus on these areas can improve customer satisfaction, increase sales, and build stronger customer relationships.
